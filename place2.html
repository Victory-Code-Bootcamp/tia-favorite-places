Waitomo Glowworm Cave, New Zealand

In this subterranean space, tiny organisms (Arachnocampa luminosa), native to New Zealand, create light patterns with their luminescent bodies.

Barton Creek Cave, Belize

This cave, once inhabited by ancient Mayan civilizations and used as a burial place, is also an important archeological site.

Harrison's Cave, Barbados

Harrison's chambers, reachable by a tramway, were hewn out of the limestone rock by water erosion over hundreds of thousands of years.
